The World Record 49x49x49 Rubik's Cube

This is the world’s highest-order Twisty Puzzle—a 49x49x49 twisty puzzle that I designed, prototyped, and assembled from scratch. The design went through several iterations and hundreds of hours of CAD work to perfect, with a focus on optimizing the internal structure for stability, flexibility, and minimal friction.

The puzzle is comprised of 13,827 individually moving pieces, and took five years to complete. I took on this project without compensation, sponsorship, or external reward. I scripted, filmed, and edited this video, which documents the entire engineering and assembly process. At the time of writing, it has garnered over 280,000 views on YouTube.

Tools used: SolidWorks, FDM 3D Printing, Manual Machining, Video Production

    Rat Vaping Nose Port Design

    At Utah State University, I was hired to work for the Odum Lab in the psychology department. This lab focused on animal research, specifically studying the behavioral and health effects of vaping in rats. I was initially brought on due to my skills in 3D printing, but the scope of my position quickly expanded to solving the myriad of engineering problems the lab faced.

    Over time, I personally designed and fabricated numerous devices and electronics for the lab. Most notably, the job of overhauling the nose port design. The nose port is a device used to train rats to use a vape. It initially dispenses sugar water to train them to use the port, and once training is complete, it switches to delivering nicotine vapors. The central idea behind the nose port is to allow rats to vape voluntarily, rather than being forced. To accomplish this, vacuum holes were placed around the mouth of the port to suck out any vapor that the rat didn’t breathe in.

    The nose port design went through 18 separate iterations. With each version adding features and resolving bugs from the previous design. In the end I wound up completely redesigning the port from the ground up twice to meet the design requirements. This process was highly collaborative—I regularly gathered feedback from the psychology researchers until we arrived at an optimized, fully functional design.

    In addition to the nose port, here are some of the other devices I designed and fabricated for this project:

    - Photo Detector Electronic Circuit

    - Solenoid Electronic Component

    - Custom Vape and Solenoid Housing

    - Custom Vacuum Tube Splitters

    - Custom Box for the Photo Detector

    - A wide variety of custom 3D-printed spacers and small widgets

    Tools used: SolidWorks, SLA (Resin) 3D Printing, FDM (plastic) 3D Printing, Electronics Prototyping, Soldering

    Avalanche Drone Explosive Release Mechanism

    This was an extremely interesting senior project I worked on along with four other mechanical engineers. The project was a contract for a company called Delta-V Energetics.

    This was my senior year capstone project in mechanical engineering. The design underwent four different iterations before settling on the final version. It was purpose-built as a gravity-powered release mechanism, which differentiated it from traditional grenade designs that carry their ignition energy at all times. This mechanism was designed so that initiation could only occur once the drone was airborne.

    The release mechanism was built to fit the chassis of a large drone, allowing the drone to fly over avalanche-prone areas in winter. The drone would release the payload to trigger controlled avalanches.

    Tools used: Fusion 360, FDM 3D Printing, CNC Machining, Manual Lathe Machining, Electronics Prototyping
